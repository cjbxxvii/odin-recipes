EASY AND QUICK HOMEMADE PIZZA

"Pizza from scratch in under an hour. Much better than the ones you buy! Use your favorite toppings."

There's nothing quite like the allure of a homemade pizza, with its perfectly golden crust, the aroma of simmering tomato sauce, and the rich, melted cheese. The magic of this particular recipe lies in the dough, handcrafted with just the right amount of yeast and allowed to rises lowly, doubling in size, which results in a crust that is the perfect blend of crispy and chewy.

Topped with a homemade tomato sauce, seasoned with a blend of herbs that have been left to marry their flavors, and a generous helping of mozzarella cheese that stretches with each slice, this pizza is a testament to the beauty of simple, fresh ingredients coming together to create a symphony of flavors.

Ingredients for the Dough

  • 2 1/4 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup warm water (about 100F)
  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Ingredients for the Sauce:

  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes or 2 cups fresh ripe tomatoes, blended
  • 2 cloves garlic minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt to taste
  • Pinch of sugar (optional)

Toppings

  • 1 1/2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Additional toppings as desired (sliced bell peppers, onions, mushrooms, pepperoni, etc)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Dough:

    • In a small bowl, dissolve the sugar and yeast in warm water and let it sit for 5 to 10 minutes until frothy.
    • In a large bowl, combine flour and salt. Make a well in the center and add the yeast to mixture and olive oil.
    • Mix until a sticky dough forms, then turn out onto a floured surface and knead for about 5 minutes until smooth and elastic.
    • Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for 1 hour or until doubled in size.

  2. Make the Sauce:

    • Heat olive oil in a saucepand over medium heat.
    • Add crushed tomatoes, oregano, basil, salt, and a pinch of sugar. Simmer for 20-30 minutes until the sauce thickens. Adjust seasoning to taste

  3. Assemble and Bake the Pizza:

    • Preheat your oven to its highest setting (450-500F) with a pizza stone or inverted baking sheet inside.
    • Punch down the dough and divide it into two balls for two pizzas, or keep it as one for a larger pizza.
    • On a piece of parchment paper, roll out the dough into a circle about 1/4 inch thick.
    • Spread a thin layer of sauce over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges.

    • Sprinkle mozzarella cheese over the sauce, and add any other toppings you like.
    • Carefully transfer the pizza (with the parchment paper) onto the preheated pizza stone or baking sheet.
    • Bake for 10-15 minutes or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly and starting to brown.
    • Remove grom the oven, let it cool for a few minutes, then slice and serve.
